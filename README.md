Sample randomization across assay plates is an important experimental design strategy to minimize systematic bias and improve data quality. In plate-based assays, including Olink proximity extension assays (PEA), mass spectrometry, HPLC, and sequencing workflows, analytical performance can be influenced by non-biological factors such as reagent preparation, operator handling, instrument drift, edge effects, processing order, and batch-to-batch variation. If samples from the same experimental group are processed together, these technical effects may become confounded with true biological differences, leading to biased results. Randomizing sample placement and processing order distributes potential sources of technical variation evenly across experimental groups, reducing the risk of systematic error and improving the accuracy, reproducibility, and statistical validity of downstream analyses.
This Python-based tool enables rapid and reproducible randomization of sample placement across one or more plates and automatically generates plate maps for laboratory use. By standardizing the randomization process, the tool reduces manual effort, minimizes transcription errors, and promotes consistent experimental design across studies.
